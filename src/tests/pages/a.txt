====Usage notes====
{{rel-top|Doubling of long A}}
* In the German-based spelling, long ''a'' is doubled to ''aa'' when the German cognate word has ''aa'' as well. Long ''a'' may or may not be doubled in the following cases:
** when it is followed by two or more consonants: {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|Saach}}, {{q|rarely}} {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|Sach}};
** when the German cognate has two vowel letters: {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|glaawe}}, {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|glawe}} (German {{m-lite|de|glauben}});
** when the German cognate has a consonant lost or not present in Central Franconian: {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|waade}}, {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|wade}} (German {{m-lite|de|warten}});
** when the German cognate has a short vowel: {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|baal}}, {{m-lite|gmw-cfr|bal}} (German {{m-lite|de|bald}}).
* In the Dutch-based spelling, long ''a'' is always doubled in closed syllables, always written simple in open syllables.
{{rel-bottom}}
